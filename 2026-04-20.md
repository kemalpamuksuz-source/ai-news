### [Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview and Project Glasswing: Restricting the Most Capable AI Yet](https://www.anthropic.com/glasswing)
**Source:** Anthropic / Fortune | **Published:** April 7, 2026
**Summary:** Anthropic unveiled Claude Mythos Preview, a frontier model with extraordinary cybersecurity capabilities—autonomously identifying thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities in every major OS and browser, including a 17-year-old remote code execution flaw in FreeBSD (CVE-2026-4747). Rather than a general release, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing: a controlled program giving access to ~50 critical infrastructure organizations (AWS, Apple, Cisco, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, JPMorganChase, CrowdStrike, and others) to defensively harden systems before such capabilities become widespread. The model will not be publicly released.
**Why it matters:** This is the clearest example yet of a lab deliberately withholding a frontier model on safety grounds—establishing a new precedent for capability-gated AI deployment.

### [New York's RAISE Act Finalized: Frontier AI Developers Face 72-Hour Incident Reporting](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/artificial-intelligence-law-advisor/2026/04/ny-overhauls-frontier-ai-transparency-law)
**Source:** Davis Wright Tremaine | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** New York Governor Hochul signed the final chapter amendments to the Responsible AI Safety and Education (RAISE) Act on March 27, 2026, with the law taking effect January 1, 2027. Frontier model developers (those using >10²⁶ FLOPs of compute) must publish safety protocols, report incidents to the state within 72 hours, and transmit catastrophic-risk assessments every three months to a new oversight office housed within the Department of Financial Services. Violations carry penalties up to $3 million.
**Why it matters:** New York's RAISE Act is the most detailed U.S. state AI safety law to date and sets a compliance baseline that will force major labs to operationalize safety reporting regardless of federal preemption debates.

### [White House Pushes Federal Preemption of All State AI Laws](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/03.20.26-National-Policy-Framework-for-Artificial-Intelligence-Legislative-Recommendations.pdf)
**Source:** The White House / Holland & Knight | **Published:** March–April 2026
**Summary:** The Trump Administration's National Policy Framework for AI, released March 20, explicitly recommends that Congress preempt state AI laws that "impose undue burdens," targeting the patchwork of state-level rules like California's SB 1047 successor bills and New York's RAISE Act.
