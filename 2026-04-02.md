### [DeepSeek's mHC Paper Called a "Striking Breakthrough" in Model Training Architecture](https://deepseek.ai/blog/deepseek-mhc-manifold-constrained-hyper-connections)
**Source:** DeepSeek AI Blog / South China Morning Post | **Published:** January 2026 (renewed attention this week)
**Summary:** DeepSeek published a paper introducing Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC), which replaces traditional residual connections in deep networks with mathematically constrained multi-stream connections that prevent training instability at scale. Tests on models up to 27B parameters showed consistent benchmark improvements (e.g., BBH score rising from 43.8 to 51.0) with only 6.7% training time overhead. Unconstrained Hyper-Connections caused catastrophic training divergence at 12,000 steps; mHC eliminates this entirely.
**Why it matters:** If adopted broadly, mHC could allow labs to scale models further and more reliably without the exponential fragility that makes large training runs expensive gambles.

### [Prime Intellect Proposes "Recursive Language Models" for Long-Horizon Agentic AI](https://www.primeintellect.ai/blog/rlm)
**Source:** Prime Intellect / arXiv | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** Researchers at Prime Intellect published a framework for Recursive Language Models (RLMs), which allow a model to actively manage its own context window by delegating information to sub-processes rather than compressing or summarizing — avoiding information loss entirely. Unlike standard chain-of-thought, RLMs integrate context management into training via reinforcement learning. The researchers argue this is the missing ingredient for AI agents capable of working autonomously on tasks spanning weeks or months.
**Why it matters:** Solving long-horizon context degradation is one of the central unsolved problems in agentic AI; RLMs offer a concrete architectural path forward.

**Source:** CNBC / White House | **Published:** March 20, 2026
**Summary:** The Trump White House released a comprehensive National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence, calling on Congress to establish a unified federal standard that would preempt "unduly burdensome" state AI laws. The six-pronged framework covers child safety, data center energy permitting, intellectual property, free speech, and workforce development. An accompanying executive order directed the Attorney General to establish an AI Litigation Task Force to challenge inconsistent state laws on interstate commerce grounds.
**Why it matters:** If enacted, this would be the first comprehensive federal AI law in US history and would override the growing patchwork of state regulations (California SB 53, Texas TRAIGA, Colorado AI Act) that companies currently must navigate.
