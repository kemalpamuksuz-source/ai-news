### [White House Releases National AI Policy Framework, Calls for Federal Preemption of State Laws](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/white-house-releases-a-national-policy-framework-for-artificial)
**Source:** Holland & Knight / White House | **Published:** March 20, 2026
**Summary:** The White House published sweeping legislative recommendations for a National AI Policy Framework, calling for a single federal standard that preempts state AI laws deemed to "impose undue burdens." The Framework advises against creating a new dedicated federal AI regulator, instead advocating for oversight through existing agencies with domain expertise. It also recommends Congress mandate child-safety and age-verification requirements for AI services.
**Why it matters:** Federal preemption, if enacted, would effectively void or override New York's RAISE Act and California's TFAIA, potentially reshaping the entire US regulatory landscape just as state-level frameworks begin to take effect.

### [New York Overhauls RAISE Act, Setting New Transparency Standards for Frontier AI Developers](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/artificial-intelligence-law-advisor/2026/04/ny-overhauls-frontier-ai-transparency-law)
**Source:** Davis Wright Tremaine | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** New York signed an amended RAISE Act in March 2026, effective January 1, 2027, imposing registration, disclosure, and fee requirements on developers of "frontier models" trained with over 10²⁶ FLOPs at cost exceeding $100M. Annual revenues must exceed $500M for the law to apply. Penalties reach $3M for repeat violations. California's comparable TFAIA is already in force as of January 1, 2026.
**Why it matters:** With two major US states now actively regulating frontier AI developers and the White House pushing federal preemption, a decisive legal confrontation over AI governance jurisdiction is building throughout 2026.

### [Neuro-Symbolic AI Slashes Robot Energy Use 100x While Raising Task Success Rate to 95%](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260405003952.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily | **Published:** April 5, 2026
**Summary:** Researchers at Tufts University demonstrated a neuro-symbolic vision-language-action (VLA) model that achieves a 95% task success rate on robotic manipulation — compared to 34% for standard neural-only systems — while consuming only 1% of training energy and 5% of inference energy. The system combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning, enabling robots to plan logically rather than rely on brute-force trial and error. The work will be presented at ICRA in Vienna in May 2026.
**Why it matters:** If the energy efficiency gains scale, this approach could decouple AI capability growth from the explosive power consumption that is currently straining global electricity infrastructure.
