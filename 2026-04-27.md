### [DeepSeek V4 Preview Is Officially Live and Open-Sourced](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/24/1136422/why-deepseeks-v4-matters/)
**Source:** MIT Technology Review | **Published:** April 24, 2026
**Summary:** DeepSeek shipped two MIT-licensed open-weight models on April 24: V4-Pro (1.6 trillion total / 49B active parameters, MoE) and V4-Flash (284B total / 13B active), both with 1-million-token context windows and a new "Hybrid Attention" architecture. V4-Pro benchmarks rival top closed-source frontier models in math and coding, while V4-Flash undercuts even OpenAI's GPT-5.4 Nano on price at $0.14/million input tokens. DeepSeek flagged the launch as a preview phase, with legacy API aliases deprecated on July 24.
**Why it matters:** A year after DeepSeek-R1 upended global AI markets, V4's combination of frontier-level capability and dramatically lower inference costs renews pressure on Western labs to justify their pricing and compute advantage.

### [EU Parliament Confirms AI Act Amendments: High-Risk Compliance Deadline Pushed to 2028, Nudifier Apps Banned](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/news/en/press-room/20260323IPR38829/artificial-intelligence-act-delayed-application-ban-on-nudifier-apps)
**Source:** European Parliament | **Published:** March 26, 2026
**Summary:** The European Parliament voted to adopt its position on the Digital Omnibus on AI, which delays mandatory compliance for high-risk AI systems from August 2026 to as late as August 2028, linking the start date to the availability of harmonised technical standards. The same package adds an outright ban on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery ("nudifier apps") as a prohibited practice. The text now moves to trilogue negotiations between Parliament, Council, and Commission.
**Why it matters:** Pushing the high-risk compliance window by up to two years gives industry significant breathing room but draws criticism from civil-society groups who argue it weakens one of the world's most ambitious AI governance frameworks at a critical moment.

### [EU Commits €63.2M to AI Innovation in Health and Online Safety](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai)
**Source:** European Commission | **Published:** April 21, 2026
**Summary:** The European Commission made €63.2 million available to support AI innovation projects targeting healthcare applications and online safety tools, part of its broader Digital Decade strategy. The funding is intended to help European SMEs and research institutions develop compliant AI systems ahead of the August 2026 full-application date for the EU AI Act. ### [AI Breakthrough Cuts Energy Use by 100× While Improving Accuracy](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260405003952.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily | **Published:** April 5, 2026
**Summary:** Researchers presented a radically more efficient computing approach that reduces AI energy consumption by up to 100 times compared to conventional GPU-based inference while simultaneously improving model accuracy on benchmark tasks. The work, highlighted during National Robotics Week, builds on physics-informed machine learning techniques that constrain model outputs to obey known physical laws, reducing the search space and therefore the compute required. **Why it matters:** A 100× energy reduction at equivalent accuracy, if reproducible at scale, would fundamentally alter the economics of AI inference and accelerate deployment in edge and embedded environments where power budgets are hard constraints.
