### [Hackers Breach Anthropic's 'Too Dangerous to Release' Mythos AI Model](https://www.euronews.com/next/2026/04/22/hackers-breach-anthropics-too-dangerous-to-release-mythos-ai-model-report)
**Source:** Euronews | **Published:** April 22, 2026
**Summary:** Anthropic's Claude Mythos, the first frontier model deemed too capable to deploy publicly, was reportedly accessed without authorization through a third-party vendor environment. The model — capable of finding and exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser without human assistance — had been restricted to 12 companies under Project Glasswing, including AWS, Apple, Microsoft, and Google. Anthropic confirmed it is "investigating a report claiming unauthorised access to Claude Mythos Preview."
**Why it matters:** This is the first time a major lab has built and withheld a completed frontier model on safety grounds, and its breach immediately tests whether restricted-deployment strategies can contain genuinely dangerous AI capabilities.

### [EU AI Act: August 2, 2026 Full-Compliance Deadline Approaching](https://www.kennedyslaw.com/en/thought-leadership/article/2026/the-eu-ai-act-implementation-timeline-understanding-the-next-deadline-for-compliance/)
**Source:** Kennedy's Law | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** The EU AI Act becomes fully applicable on August 2, 2026 — just over 14 weeks away — requiring conformity assessments, finalized technical documentation, CE marking, and EU database registration for all high-risk AI systems. The European Commission made €63.2 million available in April to support AI innovation in health and online safety, and discussions are underway on a broader Digital Omnibus that would adjust the Act's interplay with other EU laws.
**Why it matters:** The August deadline represents the most consequential AI compliance moment globally to date, affecting every company offering high-risk AI systems in Europe and setting a precedent for AI governance elsewhere.

### [AI Scientist-v2 Produces First Fully AI-Generated Paper to Pass Peer Review](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08066)
**Source:** arXiv / Sakana AI | **Published:** April 2025 (Nature publication March 2026)
**Summary:** Sakana AI's AI Scientist-v2 autonomously proposed hypotheses, ran experiments, analyzed results, and wrote a manuscript that passed the peer-review process at an ICLR workshop — the first fully AI-generated paper accepted by a rigorous academic review. The system uses a progressive agentic tree-search methodology managed by a dedicated experiment manager agent, requires no human-authored code templates, and generalizes across diverse ML domains. A paper describing the work was subsequently published in *Nature*.
**Why it matters:** Autonomous scientific discovery systems completing the full research cycle — including surviving peer review — marks a qualitative shift in AI's role from research tool to research agent.
