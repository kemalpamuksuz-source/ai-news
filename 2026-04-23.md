### [Stanford HAI Releases 2026 AI Index Report](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report)
**Source:** Stanford HAI / The Decoder | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** Stanford's annual AI Index finds AI has reached near-100% on SWE-bench Verified coding tasks (up from 60% a year ago) and frontier models gained 30 percentage points on Humanity's Last Exam — yet the same models fail to read an analog clock 50% of the time. Organizational AI adoption hit 88% globally, but public trust in government AI regulation fell to 31% in the US (last among surveyed nations), and the number of AI researchers immigrating to the US dropped 80% in a single year. Model transparency scores also declined, from 58 to 40 points on the Foundation Model Transparency Index.
**Why it matters:** The index provides the field's most comprehensive annual benchmark of AI progress, adoption, and societal risk — and the 2026 edition paints a picture of rapid capability gains shadowed by eroding trust and transparency.

### [EU AI Act Omnibus Proposes Pushing High-Risk Compliance to 2027–2028](https://www.techpolicy.press/eus-ai-act-delays-let-highrisk-systems-dodge-oversight/)
**Source:** TechPolicy.Press / OneTrust | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** The European Parliament and Council are aligned on a Digital Omnibus proposal that would delay AI Act compliance for standalone high-risk AI systems to December 2027, and for high-risk AI embedded in regulated products to August 2028 — a 24-month extension. A political deal must be reached before June 2026 to take legal effect before the original August 2 deadline. Critics from 40 human rights groups argue the delays leave the most sensitive AI applications, including biometric identification systems, permanently outside oversight.
**Why it matters:** The extension could let some of the world's most consequential AI deployments — in healthcare, law enforcement, and hiring — operate without regulatory checks for years longer than the law originally intended.

### [NVIDIA Launches Ising: First Open AI Models for Quantum Computing](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-ising-the-worlds-first-open-ai-models-to-accelerate-the-path-to-useful-quantum-computers)
**Source:** NVIDIA Newsroom | **Published:** April 14, 2026
**Summary:** On World Quantum Day, NVIDIA released Ising — the first open-source AI model family designed to accelerate fault-tolerant quantum computing. The family includes a 35B-parameter vision-language model for qubit calibration that outperforms Gemini, Claude, and GPT on the new QCalEval benchmark, and a 3D CNN-based decoder for real-time quantum error correction that is 2.5× faster and 3× more accurate than classical methods. Early adopters include Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, Harvard, and the UK National Physical Laboratory.
**Why it matters:** AI-driven quantum error correction is considered the key bottleneck before useful quantum advantage — Ising is the first open attempt to crack it at scale.
