### [Google TurboQuant: 6x KV Cache Compression With Zero Accuracy Loss (ICLR 2026)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/google-turboquant-ai-memory-compression-silicon-valley-pied-piper/)
**Source:** TechCrunch | **Published:** March 25, 2026
**Summary:** Google Research published TurboQuant, a new KV cache compression algorithm presented at ICLR 2026 that compresses the key-value cache down to 3-4 bits per element with no retraining required and no measurable accuracy degradation. On H100 GPUs, 4-bit TurboQuant delivers up to 8x performance improvement over 32-bit baselines. An open-source implementation is already live on GitHub.
**Why it matters:** By cutting LLM memory overhead by 6x without quality loss, TurboQuant could dramatically lower inference costs across the industry and enable larger context windows on existing hardware.

### [New York Governor Signs RAISE Act — First U.S. State Law Regulating Frontier AI Models](https://www.governor.ny.gov/news/governor-hochul-signs-nation-leading-legislation-require-ai-frameworks-ai-frontier-models)
**Source:** Office of Governor Kathy Hochul | **Published:** March 27, 2026
**Summary:** Governor Hochul signed the Responsible AI Safety and Education (RAISE) Act, making New York the first U.S. state to require large AI developers (over $500M in revenue) to publish safety frameworks, disclose training data practices, and report safety incidents within 72 hours. The law takes effect January 1, 2027 and creates an oversight office within the Department of Financial Services.
**Why it matters:** The RAISE Act directly confronts the Trump administration's executive order to preempt state AI regulation, setting up a major federal-vs.-state legal battle that will shape how frontier AI is governed in the United States.

**Source:** OneTrust | **Published:** April 2026
**Summary:** Following the European Parliament's March 26 plenary adoption of its position on AI Act amendments, trilogue negotiations with the Council and Commission have now begun with a target of reaching agreement by April 28. The EU's Digital Omnibus proposal extends the compliance deadline for high-risk AI systems from August 2026 to December 2, 2027, and for product-embedded systems to August 2028.
