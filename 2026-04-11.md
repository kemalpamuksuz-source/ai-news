### 5. [Google Releases Gemma 4: Most Capable Open-Source AI Family Yet](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/)
**Source:** Google Blog | **Published:** April 2, 2026
**Summary:** Google launched Gemma 4 in four sizes (2B, 4B, 26B MoE, 31B Dense) under Apache 2.0, capable of running fully offline on consumer hardware including phones and Raspberry Pi. All variants support multimodal inputs, 256K token context windows, 140+ languages, and agentic workflows. **Why it matters:** Gemma 4's offline capability and permissive license lower the barrier for on-device AI globally, extending frontier-level reasoning to edge hardware without cloud dependency.

### 6. [White House Releases National AI Policy Framework, Urges Federal Preemption of State Laws](https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/03/president-donald-j-trump-unveils-national-ai-legislative-framework/)
**Source:** The White House | **Published:** March 20, 2026 (widely analyzed April 8, 2026)
**Summary:** The Trump administration released a 13-page National Policy Framework for AI recommending that Congress establish a unified federal standard and preempt state-level AI regulations deemed "undue burdens." The framework's seven pillars span child protection, IP rights, free speech, AI workforce development, and explicit preemption of state laws. The DOJ's new AI Litigation Task Force was simultaneously authorized to challenge state AI statutes.
**Why it matters:** Federal preemption — if enacted — would nullify dozens of state AI laws already in effect, creating a single national standard that industry favors but privacy and consumer protection advocates strongly oppose.

### 9. [Neuro-Symbolic AI Slashes Energy Use 100× While Improving Accuracy](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260405003952.htm)
**Source:** ScienceDaily | **Published:** April 5, 2026
**Summary:** Researchers at Tufts University developed a neuro-symbolic AI system — combining neural networks with symbolic, rule-based reasoning — that achieves a 95% task-success rate on complex robotic challenges versus 33% for conventional vision-language models, using just 5% of operating energy and 1% of training energy.
