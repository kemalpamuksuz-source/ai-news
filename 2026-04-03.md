### [OpenAI Fully Retires GPT-4o, Completes Transition to GPT-5 Lineup](https://openai.com/index/retiring-gpt-4o-and-older-models/)
**Source:** OpenAI (official) | **Published:** April 3, 2026
**Summary:** April 3 marks the final deadline for GPT-4o's retirement across all customer tiers, including Business, Enterprise, and Edu users. **Why it matters:** This closes a major chapter in AI product history and signals that frontier AI product cycles are now moving fast enough to make even beloved, widely-used models obsolete within 18 months.

### [White House Releases National AI Policy Framework, Pushes Federal Preemption of State Laws](https://www.ropesgray.com/en/insights/alerts/2026/03/the-white-house-legislative-recommendations-national-policy-framework-for-artificial-intelligence-an)
**Source:** Ropes & Gray / White House | **Published:** March 20, 2026 (dominating policy discussions this week)
**Summary:** The Trump administration released a National Policy Framework for AI, urging Congress to establish a single federal AI oversight authority and broadly preempt conflicting state AI laws. The framework takes a "light-touch," innovation-first approach and would block states from regulating AI model development or imposing liability on AI developers for third-party misuse. Congress has so far declined to enact such comprehensive preemption in prior legislation.
**Why it matters:** If enacted, this would be the most sweeping federal intervention in AI governance in U.S. history, putting it on a direct collision course with a wave of state-level AI legislation.

### [Tennessee Signs Bill Banning AI Systems Posing as Mental Health Professionals](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-april3-2026)
**Source:** Transparency Coalition / WJHL | **Published:** April 3, 2026
**Summary:** Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee signed SB 1580, prohibiting deployment of any AI system that represents itself as a qualified mental health professional. The bill passed both chambers with near-unanimous bipartisan votes (Senate 32-0, House 94-0). Similar measures are advancing in Nebraska and Georgia as part of a nationwide wave of chatbot safety legislation following Oregon and Washington's earlier enactments.
**Why it matters:** The unanimous bipartisan support and multi-state replication pattern suggests AI companion safety laws could become standard across the U.S. regardless of federal preemption efforts.
